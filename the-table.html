The Table

Entirely Hypothetical

If We Opened Tomorrow

This is what would be on the table.

We're not announcing a location. We're not opening a restaurant. Nobody's signed a lease. This is just a menu. A thought exercise. The repertoire, written down — because people kept asking what we actually served, and honestly, it's been a while since we sat down and listed it.

Everything here was on the menu at one point. Everything was made from scratch. Everything had regulars who would drive across town for it. If a kitchen did open tomorrow — which, again, is not happening — this is what would come out of it.

If any of this sounds good, you probably already know where this goes.

The Full Repertoire

The Menu, If It Were Real.

Starters & Mezze

The small things that stopped conversations
  • The Humtziki Homemade hummus blended with our house tzatziki. Our invention. You can't unknow it once you've had it.
  • Roasted Red Pepper & Basil Hummus Freshly ground chickpeas, tahini, olive oil, roasted red peppers with basil and a whisper of mint, plus a few secrets we'll throw in when no one's looking.
  • Tzatziki, done right Strained Greek yogurt, squeezed-dry cucumber, dill, lemon, good olive oil, and exactly the right amount of garlic — which is always more than you'd expect.
  • Melitzonosalata Roasted eggplant, garlic, olive oil, spices. Smoky, silky, addictive.
  • Taramousalata A spicy hot (or mild, if you asked nicely) cheese dip made with feta and awesome.
  • Saganaki Fried cheese, lit on fire tableside. Yes, really.
  • Hand-Rolled Spanakopita One at a time, by hand, the way it's supposed to be. Other fillings available if you asked us nicely.
  • Dolmades Stuffed grape leaves with ground lamb, rice, and the spices that matter.
  • Lamb Meatballs on a Pick Fresh-ground leg of lamb, oregano, mint, basil, garlic, a little cumin, a little chili. In-house gyros if you wanted them.
  • Home-Made Falafel Spiced and rolled vegetarian magic.
  • Avgo Keftethes Egg-and-herb fritters. Everyone asked what was in them. We never fully told them.
  • Potato, Fish, or Chicken Fritters Choose your fighter. All three if you were with a group.
  • Fresh-Baked Pita Dough made from scratch on premises. Baked small for parties. Baked hot for everything else.

Off the Grill

Where the name came from
  • Grilled Lamb Souvlakia Cubes of marinated leg of lamb, kissed by open coals.
  • Grilled Chicken Souvlakia Breast in the marinade, grilled to order. Lemony, bright, herbaceous.
  • The Secret-Recipe Chicken Kabob Luxurious pieces of chicken breast, snuggled in spices and flame-grilled over charcoal.
  • Lamb Gyros House-made, end to end. Sliced from the spit, tucked into fresh pita, finished with tzatziki.
  • Greek Lamb Burger Because sometimes the bun is the point.
  • Whole Fish, Grilled Lemon, garlic, herbs — snippy, zippy, simple. Served bone-in because that's how you get flavor.
  • Grilled or Roasted Octopus Sleek with olive oil and awesome.
  • Country-Style Rib Kabobs Skewered, charred, impossible to eat without standing up.
  • The Grillin' Greek Wing, Drumstick, Breast Our rubs. Our coals. Your night.
  • Sesame BBQ Chicken Sticks Sticky. On a stick. A crowd favorite nobody asked us to stop making.
  • Glazed Chicken Teriyaki Not traditional. Didn't matter. People wouldn't stop ordering it.

Out of the Oven

Slow, patient, impossible to rush
  • Roasted Lamb Leg Stuffed with fresh garlic, mint, and basil. Carved at the table when the occasion called for it.
  • Roasted Baby Lamb Tender, melting, mouthwatering. Don't try to have a conversation for the first 45 minutes.
  • Roasted Greek-Style Ribs Addictive. That's the whole description.
  • Lamb Chops & Greek Sliced Potatoes The closer. Served rare to medium-rare because we were not going to apologize for that.
  • Roasted Chicken, Any Part, Any Way Curry. Garlic. Urfa chili. Whatever mood the kitchen was in. All of it good.
  • Roasted Rosemary Turkey Any part. Whole bird. Up to you, up to the season.
  • Moussaka Eggplant lasagna, layered, béchamel-blanketed. The right way.
  • Pastichio Greek lasagna. Pasta, meat, béchamel. Goodnight.
  • Stuffed Peppers, Zucchini, or Eggplant Same honest stuffing, different vessel. Pick your vegetable.
  • Stuffed Cabbage Ground lamb, rice, the spices that matter. Rolled by hand.
  • Papoutsakia Stuffed eggplant halves. The name means "little shoes." We know.
  • Circle of Plant Grilled eggplant rounds stuffed with awesome and cheese. Sounds made up. Isn't.
  • Slow-Cooked Brisket All the time in the world. Pulls apart with a fork and an opinion.
  • Franzolla Greek-style meatloaf. If that helps.
  • Smoked Meats Whichever kind. Whenever the mood took us. Ask and find out.

The Great Salads

What kept the salad crowd loyal
  • The Great Greek Salad Tomatoes, cucumbers, arguing, romaine, philosophy, onions, friends, red peppers, olives, love, and feta. And feta. And feta.
  • Horyiatiki — the Village Version No romaine. We promise. Just the good stuff: tomato, cucumber, onion, olive, pepper, and a brick of feta on top.
  • Grilled Greek Salad The signature. Fresh greens, your choice of grilled marinated chicken or thinly sliced seasoned lamb — hot off the grill, straight onto the cold greens. Mom's dressing, always.
  • Tabouli Bright, green, technically a side. Regulars ordered it as a main anyway.
  • Summer Pasta Salad Greek-style or regular. There was no bad answer.
  • Spanakorizo Spinach rice. Herbal, simple, hits like a home-cooked memory.

From the Sea

Fried, grilled, or slow-simmered
  • Fried Calamari Golden. Crisp. Gone in five minutes. Served with lemon and the house tzatziki.
  • Slow-Cooked Calamari in Spicy Sauce The other school of thought. Tender, tomato-forward, fiery.
  • Stuffed Calamari Because someone was always going to ask.
  • Fried Shrimp · Scallops · Clams The platter. Always a good idea, any season.
  • Striped Bass, Grilled or Roasted A flurry of herbs, garlic, and fresh breadcrumbs. Served whole for the people who know what to do with a whole fish.
  • Seafood Soup Brothy, layered, full of whatever came in that day.
  • Seafood Stew (Spicy) Not for the meek. Served with bread for the broth.

Soups & Stews

The slow ones, the honest ones
  • Chicken Soup & Chicken Noodle The classic. Made the classic way.
  • Bean, Lentil, or Split Pea Pick whichever cold day it happens to be.
  • Rib n' Bean Soup The Sunday-afternoon special.
  • Yiouvarlakia Lamb and rice meatballs in a gentle lemon broth. Quiet power.
  • Lamb Stew Slow-cooked lamb, caramelized onions, garlic, fresh ground tomato, the spices of the night.
  • Green Bean Stew Fasolakia. Olive oil-forward. Served warm or room temperature.

The Comforts

Pastas, potatoes, and the late-night heroes
  • Spicy Garlic Mashed Potatoes The house default. The upgrade nobody regretted.
  • Sausage or Brisket Spicy Garlic Mashed The regular's upgrade. Only on the board for people who knew to ask.
  • Baked Mashed Potatoes Yes, baked. Yes, it's a different thing. Yes, it's better.
  • Makaronia me Kema Greek meat sauce over pasta. Cinnamon-warm, slow-built, feels like home.
  • Makaronia, Teri, ke Avga Greek mac and cheese. Don't overthink it.
  • Rice Pilaf Done properly. Toasted grains, real broth, patience.
  • Spicy Greek Tacos Yes, we did that. Yes, it worked.
  • Crepe Roll Wraps Stuffed with whichever meat and vegetable were calling to us that day.
  • Greek Meat & Cheese Nachos The late-night hero. Order it and argue with whoever you're with about something unimportant.
  • Fried Potatoes & Cheese Melt Every ingredient working overtime.
  • Lamb Burger & Tzatziki Melt A cheeseburger with ideas. Served on fresh bread.

The Sauces & The Dressings

The quiet engines behind everything else
  • Mom's Greek Dressing The family recipe. The reason the salads were different. Never sold. Until maybe now.
  • The Humtziki Our invention, in a jar (someday). The hummus-tzatziki hybrid you'd put on everything.
  • Tzatziki The benchmark. Made the right way, or not at all.
  • Feta Tzatziki Dip For when tzatziki alone wasn't enough.
  • Traditional Spicy Salsa — "Over Salad" Named accordingly. Sits on the table, refuses to be ignored.

From the Files

Actual menus. Actual dinners. Actual evidence.

Over the years we catered a lot of dinner parties. Private homes. Holiday gatherings. Corporate evenings. Some of those menus survived — handwritten, printed, emailed, stapled inside recipe notebooks. Here's two of them, verbatim. Note the descriptions. That's how we talked about the food then. Still do.
